Capital Efficiency

The Dynamic Market Maker (DMM) achieves 200× capital efficiency by concentrating all liquidity within a narrow band around the reflective price, rather than spreading it across all possible prices like traditional AMMs.

Mathematical Foundation

The DMM implements constant product within a restricted range:

[Pcenter−δ,  Pcenter+δ]\left[P_{\text{center}} - \delta,\; P_{\text{center}} + \delta\right][Pcenter​−δ,Pcenter​+δ]

Where:

  • PcenterP_{\text{center}}Pcenter​ = current reflective price
  • δ\deltaδ = band half-width (e.g., 25 basis points = 0.25%)

Within this range, the traditional constant product formula applies:

x⋅y=kx \cdot y = kx⋅y=k

Where xxx and yyy represent token reserves and kkk is the invariant.

For a constant product AMM x⋅y=kx \cdot y = kx⋅y=k within a price range [Pmin⁡,Pmax⁡][P_{\min}, P_{\max}][Pmin​,Pmax​], the liquidity LLL is related to reserves by:

L=x⋅y=kL = \sqrt{x \cdot y} = \sqrt{k}L=x⋅y​=k​

In a full-range AMM (Pmin⁡=0P_{\min} = 0Pmin​=0, Pmax⁡=∞P_{\max} = \inftyPmax​=∞), the relationship between price PPP and reserves is:

P=yx⇒x=kP,  y=k⋅PP = \frac{y}{x} \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = \sqrt{\frac{k}{P}},\; y = \sqrt{k \cdot P}P=xy​⇒x=Pk​​,y=k⋅P​

Full-Range AMM:

Lfull=K2PL_{\text{full}} = \frac{K}{2\sqrt{P}}Lfull​=2P​K​

The virtual reserves at price boundaries are:

xvirt=LP(1+δ),yvirt=L⋅P(1−δ)x_{\text{virt}} = \frac{L}{\sqrt{P(1+\delta)}}, \quad y_{\text{virt}} = L \cdot \sqrt{P(1-\delta)}xvirt​=P(1+δ)​L​,yvirt​=L⋅P(1−δ)​

Recentering Mechanism

The DMM automatically re-centers its liquidity band when the market price deviates significantly from the reflective price.

Recentering Process

StepAction
1. Trigger DetectionMonitor deviation between market and reflective price
2. Fee Coverage ValidationEnsure accumulated fees cover impermanent loss
3. Band RecenteringShift liquidity to new price range centered on reflective price
4. State UpdateUpdate internal accounting and emit events

Default Trigger: 25 basis points (0.25%) deviation, matching the band half-width.

Impermanent Loss Analysis

Each band recentering operation incurs impermanent loss proportional to the square of the price movement:

IL≈δ22IL \approx \frac{\delta^2}{2}IL≈2δ2​

For δ=0.25%\delta = 0.25\%δ=0.25%, this represents approximately 0.03% of TVL per recentering event.

The fee accumulation requirement ensures:

Feesaccumulated≥φ⋅ILprojected\text{Fees}_{\text{accumulated}} \geq \varphi \cdot IL_{\text{projected}}Feesaccumulated​≥φ⋅ILprojected​

Where φ\varphiφ is the fee coverage multiplier (default: 1.0).
